Trae vs Antigravity vs Cursor: The Battle of AI IDEs in 2026

By TheGenAI Team • March 29, 2026

If you’re a developer today, you’ve probably felt it: the ground is shifting under your feet. The days of "just an editor" are long gone. We’ve moved from basic syntax highlighting to AI assistants that can practically read our minds—or at least our entire repositories. But with so many options hitting the market, which one should you actually commit your workflow to?

We spent the last month living in three different environments: Cursor (the reigning champ), Trae (the ByteDance-backed speedster), and Antigravity (Google's agent-first newcomer). Here’s our honest take on how they stack up in the areas that actually matter: your wallet, your time, and the quality of the code they produce.

1. The Financial Factor: Subscriptions vs. Efficiency

Let’s talk money first. It’s not just about the monthly subscription fee; it’s about the return on investment (ROI) you get from every hour you spend in the chair.

  • Cursor: At $20/month for Pro, it’s the standard. It feels like a utility bill now. You pay for the stability and the best-in-class models (Claude 3.5 Sonnet, GPT-4o) without having to manage your own API keys.
  • Trae: This is where it gets interesting. Trae is currently offering a robust free tier that feels suspiciously like Cursor Pro. If you’re a solo dev or a startup watching every Euro, Trae is the clear financial winner for now.
  • Antigravity: Being Google’s playground, it’s currently free in public preview. They want your data and your feedback more than your money. It’s a great way to access Gemini 3 Pro without the enterprise price tag.
Verdict: Trae wins on raw cost, but Cursor wins on predictable value for professional teams.

2. Speed of Development: Flow vs. Delegation

Speed isn't just about how fast the AI generates code; it's about how little it breaks your "flow."

Cursor is built for the "flow-state" developer. The inline autocomplete is so fast it feels like it’s predicting your thoughts. The "Composer" mode allows you to refactor entire modules in seconds while keeping you firmly in the driver’s seat.

Trae feels like a direct response to Cursor. It’s snappy, the indexing is incredibly fast, and it doesn't suffer from the occasional "lag" that Cursor sometimes hits on massive monorepos. It’s built for velocity.

Antigravity, however, is a different beast entirely. It’s not just an assistant; it’s an agent. You don't just "code faster"; you delegate tasks. You tell Antigravity, "Build a contact form that saves to Firestore and sends a Slack notification," and it goes off, plans the steps, writes the code, and tests it in a built-in browser. For greenfield prototyping, Antigravity’s "outcome-based" speed is unmatched.

3. Quality of Development: Precision vs. Autonomy

This is where things get subjective. Does the AI write "clever" code that you have to fix later, or does it write the code you would have written yourself?

Cursor still feels like it has the best "taste." Because it uses highly tuned models with deep repository context, the suggestions usually match your existing architectural patterns. It rarely suggests something that feels "out of place."

Trae is impressively accurate but occasionally leans towards more generic implementations. It’s perfect for 90% of tasks, but for that last 10% of complex architecture, you might find yourself doing a bit more manual cleanup.

Antigravity is the most "autonomous," which is a double-edged sword. When it works, it feels like magic. When it misses, it can create a mess of files that takes 20 minutes to untangle. It’s brilliant for exploration, but you’ll want to keep a close eye on it for production builds.

The Final Scoreboard

Feature Cursor Trae Antigravity
Cost $$ (Paid) Free (Currently) Free (Preview)
Best For Daily Professional Work Rapid, Cost-Effective Dev Autonomous Prototyping
"Wow" Factor Perfect Autocomplete Raw Performance Multi-Agent Tasks

Conclusion: Which one should you pick?

If you want the tool everyone trusts and you don't mind the $20/month, Cursor is still the king. It’s polished, reliable, and just works.

If you’re looking for a free alternative that doesn't compromise on speed, switch to Trae today. You’ll be surprised how close it gets to the Cursor experience. If you're ready to start building, check out our practical guide to building an 8-hour MVP.

But if you want to see the future of "agentic" development—where you manage a team of AI agents rather than writing every line yourself—Antigravity is the one to watch. It’s raw, it’s exciting, and it’s changing the definition of what an IDE can be.

At TheGenAI, we use all three depending on the project. For our 8-hour prototypes, we often lean on Antigravity for the initial scaffold and Cursor for the final production polish. The best tool is the one that stays out of your way and helps you build amazing things.
